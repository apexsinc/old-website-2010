Vehicle Conflicts and Exclusions

There are a few vehicles in which DriveRight 600E OBD does not function properly, and there are others in which DriveRight 600E OBD may interfere with the normal operation of the vehicle. Please review the list of known Vehicle Conflicts and Exclusions before purchasing or using DriveRight 600 OBD.

Important Safety Notice

DriveRight 600E OBD has been carefully designed and tested to comply with the OBDII protocols used on most cars and light trucks sold in the USA, model-year 1996 or later. However, some vehicle models are not in full compliance with these protocols. In addition, the computer control systems on any given vehicle may be malfunctioning or out of spec, as may be the sensors used by these systems.

Before installing DriveRight 600E OBD, be sure to review the list of known vehicle exclusions and anomalies (see the link above). For more information on OBDII issues and anomalies in general, we recommend The Equipment and Tool Institute's website at www.etools.org.

While our testing and the experience of thousands of DriveRight users have shown the unit to be safe and reliable, there is an inherent risk in adding any aftermarket product that may potentially affect the operation or drivability of your vehicle. Should you be concerned about the operation of your vehicle at any time while using DriveRight, you should pull over, off the roadway, immediately or as soon as it is safe to do so. Remove DriveRight from the OBDII port and consult a licensed mechanic or automobile service center.
